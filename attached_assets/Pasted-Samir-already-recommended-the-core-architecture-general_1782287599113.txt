True incremental recommendations from Sandra and Jennifer

These are the better incremental recommendations because they came out of their feedback after Samir’s architecture:

1. Explicitly account for both health-plan and CMS response paths

Sandra pointed out that the architecture should explicitly include both health-plan responses and CMS responses through the payer between submission and reconciliation. This is an incremental refinement to the diagram and language, not a brand-new architecture concept.

2. Call out response-file completeness as a dependency

Sandra specifically wanted the assessment to call out that Mercy is not consistently receiving response files from all payers. She quantified that Mercy receives MAO-004 from four of seven payers and MAO-002 from only one.

3. Treat Jennifer’s Databricks work as an interim prototype, not the final model

Jennifer confirmed her current dashboard and reconciliation work is valuable, but interim. It depends on manually receiving files, moving them to storage, running ingestion pipelines, refreshing dashboards, and working around limited access for operations users.

4. Move operational reporting to an accessible layer

Jennifer noted that operations users do not have Databricks access, so reporting likely needs to move to Power BI or another accessible layer. This is a good incremental recommendation because it is about adoption and operating model, not just architecture.

5. Add an operational review / approval workflow

Jennifer raised that Linda and Aaron currently send extracts to the operational team for validation, and she would like that process automated rather than manual. Samir reframed this as a report-based review and “blessing” process that could later become an application workflow.

6. Eventually validate against Clarity, not only submitted sweep files

Samir asked whether reconciliation should also look back to the original Clarity source to determine whether eligible records were missed entirely. Jennifer acknowledged that they are not there yet, but agreed it is something Mercy should evaluate later.

7. Consider payer technical-spec validation as a future enhancement

Sandra asked whether current payer technical specifications could be integrated between layout conversion and QA validation to confirm alignment with the latest payer specs. She also acknowledged this is more of a later “run” step than an immediate “walk” step.
